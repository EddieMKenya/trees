About Our Initiative

In the heart of Kenya, where communities face the double threat of climate change and economic hardship, a single fruit tree can mean more than shade — it can mean a family's livelihood, a child's meal, and a cleaner atmosphere for generations to come.

Trees for Planet, Profit, and Future is a community-driven initiative with one bold goal: to plant one million fruit trees across Kenya by 2025. This project will restore degraded lands, sequester thousands of tonnes of CO₂, and create sustainable livelihoods for women and youth — all while improving food security and biodiversity.

We invite sponsors to join us in this transformative journey. Together, we can make Kenya greener, communities stronger, and our planet healthier.

Our Alignment with UN Sustainable Development Goals

The Challenges We Face

Climate Vulnerability

Kenya faces increased droughts, heatwaves, and floods due to declining forest cover, making communities more vulnerable to climate shocks.

Food Insecurity

Millions lack consistent access to nutritious food as agricultural lands degrade and traditional food sources diminish.

Economic Instability

Rural communities depend on unsustainable practices like charcoal burning for income, further exacerbating environmental degradation.

The Global Impact

Globally, deforestation contributes to 10–15% of greenhouse gas emissions. Without urgent action, Kenya's ecosystems — and the communities that depend on them — will face irreversible damage. Our initiative addresses these challenges head-on with sustainable, community-centered solutions.

Our Three-Pillar Solution

For Planet

Restore biodiversity, improve soil quality, and sequester CO₂ through strategic tree planting across degraded lands in Kenya.

For Profit

Create sustainable income streams by selling fruits, decorative plants, and seedlings to generate income for local communities.

For Future

Ensure that future generations inherit a greener, healthier, and more resilient planet through environmental education and sustainable practices.

Our Project Objectives

Nursery Establishment

Establish a 10 million seedling nursery in Kajiado County with modern irrigation systems.

Vegetation Cover

Achieve 10% tree & vegetation cover in target counties to combat desertification.

Economic Empowerment

Create sustainable income for at least 500 women and youth by 2025 through agroforestry.

Environmental Education

Raise environmental awareness in 50 schools and communities through our "Adopt a Tree" program.

Carbon Sequestration

Sequester 200 tonnes of CO₂ annually from fruit trees planted in Year 1 of the project.
